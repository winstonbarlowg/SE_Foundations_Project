Project H

A data visualisation project to raise awareness about human trafficking worldwide.

The Counter Trafficking Data Collaborative (CTDC), is the first global data hub on human trafficking. It has allowed organisations and projects to better work on meaningful data analysis and visualisations to both raise awarenes and develop solutions.
From the CTDC Global Dataset, Project H looks into 55,000+ cases from 2002-2018* to draw insights for impact.

A age-broad global issue

Victims from all ages and backgrounds are trafficked worldwide. The main differences between age groups are the recruitment, means of control used, and type of exploitation

*8733 cases are from unknown age groups.

How can this happen?

Overall, victims trafficked for sexual exploitation are recruited most often by an intimate partner or friends and family. For labour exploitation, the recruiter relationship remains largely unknown (other), with a small percentage of friends and family involved.
